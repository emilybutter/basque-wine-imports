Producer · 01

Gorka Izagirre

In the hills above Bilbao, just fifteen minutes from the city, the Izagirre family farms more than forty hectares of steep, southeast-facing vineyards — each one rooted in old-vine Hondarrabi Zuri, Zerratia, and Beltza. Cool Atlantic air, constant drizzle, and a sea breeze off the Bay of Biscay shape every bottle.

Quietly opinionated, generously poured, and unmistakably of the place. We brought back four bottles.

Producer · 02

Zudugarai

On the cliffs above Getaria, the small Basque fishing village that gives the Getariako Txakolina DO its name, Zudugarai works steep coastal vineyards within sight of the Bay of Biscay. The estate is family-run, generally closed to the public, and tightly focused on three expressions of txakoli — a still white, a saline rosé, and a serious traditional-method sparkler.

Salt air, native yeasts, and a winemaker who picks one morning before sunrise. We brought back two cases of each.

The grapes

Three indigenous varieties

Native Atlantic grapes that grow in small, concentrated clusters — brimming with character, and only here. Each variety has its own field notes on the journal.

White

Hondarrabi Zuri

The Atlantic white. Fresh, joyful, saline — green apple and a thread of acidity that makes everything else on the table taste better. The grape behind every classic txakoli.

Read the field note →
White

Hondarrabi Zerratia

The quieter sibling. Elegant, voluminous, complex — with notes of white flowers, baked apple, and the longest finish on the tasting bench. Often barrel-aged.

Read the field note →
Red

Hondarrabi Beltza

Fruity, deep, herbaceous, exclusive. The coastal red that almost vanished — and that Gorka Izagirre has more of than anyone else on the planet. About six hectares, worldwide.
